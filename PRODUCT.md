# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Andrés Vargas Castillo, ULACIT student in San José, Costa Rica. Built this because ULACIT suspended physical ID issuance, leaving students without institutional identification for museums, discounts, and daily use. Primary user is the builder himself; secondary users are other ULACIT students and anyone needing a quick digital ID for any institution (via the free generator). Users open this on iPhone in Safari, add it to their home screen, and use it in the moment they need to show a student ID.

## Product Purpose

Generate a credible, exportable digital student ID. Three flows: a login-protected personal carnet (admin.html), a fixed ULACIT-template generator for any ULACIT student, and a fully customizable free generator for any institution. Success means the exported PNG looks indistinguishable from an official institutional card.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Minimal · Trusted. Quiet confidence. The card is the product — the surrounding UI is infrastructure, not decoration. Voice is functional and direct.

## Anti-references

- Government/institutional: heavy borders, stiff layouts, bureaucratic form aesthetics
- Bootstrap default: undesigned, generic framework look, visible scaffolding
- Generic SaaS: hero metrics, dashboard-widget cards, "modern" purple that looks like every other SaaS

## Design Principles

1. **The card is the hero.** Every UI element exists to direct attention to the card, not to compete with it. Reduce chrome to minimum.
2. **Precision over personality.** No decorative elements that don't do a job. Spacing, type, and color are tools, not gestures.
3. **Dark with intent.** Dark background makes the card read as a physical object under ambient light — this is functional, not fashionable.
4. **Trust through restraint.** The less the interface shouts, the more credible the output looks. Understatement signals confidence.
5. **Craft is visible.** This was built by one person for a real problem. That intention should show in the details — tight type, deliberate color, no filler.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Keyboard navigable forms. No formal reduced-motion requirement but avoid gratuitous animation.
